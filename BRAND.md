# The Number Crunch brand

## Brand idea

**The exploded view:** take a system apart, label the useful pieces, show what broke, then put it back together.

This comes from Ashutosh's habit of opening toys and computers to understand them. It fits production AI work better than generic futurism: curious, technical, practical, and willing to show the mess between a demo and a dependable system.

## Primary theme: Signal & Ink

Use this as the shared identity across websites, profiles, diagrams, decks, and social graphics.

| Role | Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Background | Cool notebook | `#F5F7FA` | Pages, slides, social backgrounds |
| Primary ink | Carbon | `#172129` | Text, wordmark, dark blocks |
| Functional accent | Lab blue | `#255CD6` | Links, charts, navigation state |
| Human accent | Markup coral | `#E6553B` | Annotations, emphasis, avatar detail |
| Evidence accent | Calibration teal | `#26776D` | Results, metadata, secondary chart series |
| Neutral | Graphite | `#65717C` | Captions and supporting text |

Recommended ratio: **70% notebook, 20% carbon, 7% lab blue, 3% coral/teal**. Coral is a mark made by a person, not a general-purpose button color.

## Type

- Headings and interface: **IBM Plex Sans**, 500–700.
- Reading: **Source Serif 4**, 400–700.
- Data, labels, and code: **IBM Plex Mono**, 400–500.

The contrast is intentional: engineered structure around human explanation.

## Wordmark

- Use the full **The Number Crunch** wordmark. Do not force it into a `TNC` monogram.
- Pair it with the custom number-sign mark when space allows.
- The separated coral bar represents a component pulled out for inspection.
- Keep clear space around the mark equal to roughly one horizontal-bar thickness.
- Minimum digital size: 24px. Use the simplified favicon below 32px.
- Keep the mark Carbon, Cool Notebook, and Markup Coral; do not recolor individual pieces.
- Use connectors only when they encode a real sequence, dependency, or data relationship.
- Labels should describe real structure; avoid decorative `01 / 02 / 03` numbering.
- Do not use glowing brains, circuit-board portraits, neon gradients, floating glass cards, or stock robot imagery.

## Utility rule

Every visible element must provide at least one of: navigation, state, hierarchy, data, explanation, or brand recognition. Remove anything else.

- Never draw controls, progress tracks, endpoints, or handles that do not respond to input.
- Use dividers only to separate or associate content.
- Use color to encode meaning or brand identity, not to fill empty space.
- Animation must explain a state change; never animate for ambience alone.
- A control label must say what happens: `Send message`, not `Send` or `Submit`.

## Platform use

- Personal avatar: use Ashutosh's portrait. Publication avatar: use `social-avatar-1200.png` or `linkedin-logo-300.png`.
- Social header: notebook field, full wordmark, and one real architecture diagram or concrete claim.
- Slides: carbon titles, blue links/data, coral annotations, teal evidence/results.
- Charts: blue primary series, teal comparison, coral only for exceptions or failures.
- Other websites: keep the type and Carbon/Lab blue constant; choose notebook, sage, or blueprint as the environment.

## Voice

Specific, candid, and useful. Prefer “Here is what failed in production” over “Unlock the future of AI.” Show assumptions. Admit uncertainty. Explain the mechanism before making the claim.
