When I was in my undergrad, I remember there was a time when front-end engineers used to think that their job can never be automated. Their job involves creativity, new thoughts, and ideas, and a keen sense of knowing what works and what doesn't. This was upended in 2023 after the advent of ChatGPT when a lot of the front-end code could be easily written using AI tools.

I was never a front-end engineer to begin with. Since I got a job this February as a software engineer at Skypad, I have worked in many hats - as an AI engineer, as a front-end engineer, as well as a back-end engineer. The time when I got the most 'meaningful' help using these AI tools was when I was working as a front-end engineer.
